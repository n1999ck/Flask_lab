About Me

Hey, I'm Nick. I'm a student at Columbus State University in Georgia working on a dual degree: a B.S. in computer science with a focus on web development and a B.A. in art history. I'm also editor-in-chief of CSU's newsmagazine, The Uproar.

As a web developer, I'm interested in full-stack and enjoy the design process. I designed and built trackrightfarm.com for my mom's business as well as a portfolio site for my first internet programming class. The languages I've worked the most with include Java, HTML/CSS/JS with Bootstrap, and Python. I've been using Ubuntu daily since 2021 and am quite comfortable with Linux terminal work.

As an art historian, I mostly focus on Western art from the nineteenth century onwards. I like to research art about human experiences like (homo)sexuality and death as well as art by Southern or Black artists. I've also worked on basic curation, exhibit installation, artist interviews, and exhibition reviews.

As editor-in-chief at The Uproar, I essentially run the publication and write articles as time allows. The job includes everything from reporting on events, performing basic photojournalism, editing others' work, managing projects and staff, collaborating with designers, illustrators, and photographers, and promoting the paper on campus. It keeps me busy but gives me abundant learning opportunities and connections.

With my spare time, I do a lot of reading, collect obsolete technology including CDs and calculators, and study whatever topics catch my interest, usually paleoanthropology.
